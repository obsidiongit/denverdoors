5 Signs It's Time to Replace Your Interior Doors

Interior doors are often overlooked when it comes to home improvement. We look at them every day, but often don't truly see them until they stop working correctly or we realize how much they date our home. If you've been on the fence about upgrading, here are five clear signs that it's time to replace your interior doors.

1. They Are Hollow Core "Builder Grade"

If your doors feel light as a feather and transmit every sound from the hallway into your bedroom, you likely have hollow core doors. These standard "builder grade" options offer minimal soundproofing and durability. Upgrading to solid core doors immediately adds structural weight and significantly dampens noise, making your home quieter and more private.

2. Visible Damage or Warping

Over time, doors can take a beating. Cracks, holes, and peeling veneer are not just unsightly; they can compromise the door's integrity. Furthermore, settling foundations or humidity shifts can cause doors to warp, preventing them from closing flush against the stop. While some sticking can be planed down, a warped door is often best replaced.

3. The Style is Stuck in the 90s

Nothing dates a home faster than flat-panel oak doors with shiny brass hardware. If you've updated your flooring and paint but kept the old doors, your renovation will feel incomplete. Modern styles like 2-panel square shaker or 5-panel horizontal doors provide a clean, contemporary look that instantly modernizes a hallway.

4. They Don't Latch or Close Properly

A door that drifts open on its own (ghosting) or requires a hip-check to latch is a daily annoyance. While sometimes this is a hinge adjustment issue, often it's a sign that the jamb itself is twisted or the door was poorly hung originally. Installing a pre-hung unit ensures perfect alignment and smooth operation.

5. Light Gaps Around the Edges

If you can see light streaming through the top or sides of your closed door, you're losing both sound insulation and privacy. This often happens when doors are cut down too much to clear new carpet, or when the original installation was not plumb. A new, custom-fitted door solves these gaps.

Transform Your Home

Replacing your interior doors is one of the most impactful upgrades you can make for both aesthetics and livability. It's a "whole home" change that elevates every room simultaneously.
